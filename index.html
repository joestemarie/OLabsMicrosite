A Mayor’s Taskforce would give college students a voice

The taskforce will bring students together to tackle common and unique issues at the District’s various institutions of higher education. The body will open a dialogue on how students across the city interface with their community and the District as a whole.
  • Representation: The taskforce will represent diverse perspective through a membership comprised of students from as many District institutions as possible; there are at least nine institutions in Washington, DC, not to mention all of those in the metro area.
  • Direct to the Mayor: Two years ago students stepped up to the plate and elected a record number of student ANC Commissioners. But students need direct access to the Mayor to achieve the representation that they seek.
  • Institutionalization: The taskforce will exist for two years, and one of its charges will be to identify a long-term mechanism for student representation to District government. Institutionalizing this interface is critical when the student population is transient.

College students are a significant slice of Washington, DC

Over 85,000 students attend school in the District, and many stay after graduating. While here, students complete hundreds of thousands of hours of community service and are active contributors to the local economy.

  • Public Service: Students have made recent strides in their public service to the District, including nearly ten ANC Commissioners elected in 2012 - a huge increase from previous participation - and appointments to local boards and commissions.
  • Community Service: The Consortium of Universities of the Washington Metropolitan Area reports that university community members completed over 600,000 hours of community service in the region last year.
  • Students are Staying: Washington, DC has become one of the top metropolitan areas for graduates, including those who attend school here. Businessweek ranked DC #19 just five years ago, but the District is the now the number two contender - and that’s not changing anytime soon.
